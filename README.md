Deep Image Prior has emerged as a promising unsupervised approach for image restoration (IR) by leveraging the intrinsic \textit{spectral bias} of untrained convolutional neural networks (CNNs) architecture. However, DIP often struggles with limited representational capacity and pronounced susceptibility to noise overfitting. In this paper, we propose Net2Net, a unified explicit-implicit dual-prior architecture that synergistically integrates an untrained deep structural prior with a pretrained deep denoising prior within regularization by denoising (RED) framework. By embedding the RED regularizer, defined as the inner product between the image and its denoising residual, into the DIP objective, we introduce an explicit, differentiable regularization term that complements the implicit architectural prior. Crucially, we provide formal validations demonstrating that the deep denoising prior employed in Net2Net satisfies the three required RED conditions: differentiability, homogeneity, and Jacobian symmetry. This foundation ensures that the RED gradient simplifies to the denoising residual, enabling the resulting objective to be efficiently solved via stable fixed-point method. Extensive experiments on image inpainting, super-resolution, and deblurring demonstrate that Net2Net consistently outperforms state-of-the-art DIP-related methods. Furthermore, evaluations on real-world datasets reveal that Net2Net achieves competitive fidelity and robustness.
